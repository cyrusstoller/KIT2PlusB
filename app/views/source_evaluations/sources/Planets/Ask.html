What makes a planet habitable?

Best Answer - Chosen by Asker

I can only give you the conditions on which life like on earth could exist.

  • A temperature of between ~3 and ~70 degrees celsius on average.
  • A star with a size between 0.5 and 1.5 times that of the sun.
  • A single star (sorry, it can't be a binary star: the orbit would be unstable).
  • A planet weight of between ~0.7 and ~4 times that of the earth
  • An atmosphere.
  • An electromagnetic field protecting it.
  • An old age.

  1. All life on Earth requires water: It is the main ingredient in all living cells, it carries dissolved nutrients, and it serves as an internal transport medium. Life on other planets may not use water, but large amounts of some sort of liquid (possibly ammonia) would be needed.
  2. The planet's temperature must allow for the continuous existence of whatever liquid the native life is based on. This requires a nearly circular orbit around the host star or a nearly constant internal heat source such as radioactive decay or tidal friction (as on some of the moons of Jupiter and Saturn).
  3. An atmosphere dense enough to keep the liquid from boiling away is also required.
  4. Life requires energy. On Earth, that energy comes from the sun or from Earth's own internal heat, as is found around deep-sea hydrothermal vents. Any alien life will require similar sources of energy.
  5. Life requires stable conditions to form. Heavy asteroid bombardment or intense planet-wide volcanic activity could destroy the complex molecules needed for life.
  6. Life on Earth requires a large number of trace elements as well as the "Big Four" of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen. Hydrogen is by far the most common element in the universe. Carbon dioxide (CO2), ammonia (NH3), water (H2O), and other simple molecules are also plentiful. Heavy elements are distributed more unevenly, however.
Free oxygen in the atmosphere is not a requirement. The oxygen in our atmosphere is a by-product of life; there was life before there was oxygen in the air. A moon or moons could help stabilize the planet's rotational axis, preventing extreme seasonal changes.
